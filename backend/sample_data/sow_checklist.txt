# Statement of Work (SoW) Audit Checklist

This checklist defines the validation criteria for reviewing Statement of Work documents.

## Document Structure & Completeness

1. The SoW must include a clear project title and identification number
2. An executive summary must be present highlighting key project objectives
3. The document must contain a detailed scope of work section
4. Project deliverables must be explicitly listed and described
5. Success criteria and acceptance criteria must be clearly defined

## Project Details

6. Project objectives and business goals must be documented
7. The target audience or stakeholders must be identified
8. Project constraints (budget, timeline, resources) must be specified
9. Assumptions and dependencies must be documented
10. Risks and mitigation strategies must be outlined

## Technical Requirements

11. Functional requirements must be clearly specified
12. Non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability) must be documented
13. Technology stack and tools to be used must be listed
14. Integration requirements with existing systems must be specified
15. Data requirements and data flow must be documented

## Timeline & Milestones

16. Project start and end dates must be specified
17. Major milestones and their target completion dates must be defined
18. A high-level project schedule or timeline must be included
19. Phase-wise breakdown of activities must be provided
20. Review and approval gates must be identified

## Resources & Responsibilities

21. Team composition and roles must be defined
22. Client responsibilities and commitments must be specified
23. Vendor/contractor responsibilities must be clearly outlined
24. Communication plan and escalation procedures must be documented
25. Reporting requirements and frequency must be specified

## Commercial Terms

26. Pricing model and cost breakdown must be included
27. Payment terms and milestones must be specified
28. Change management process must be documented
29. Intellectual property and confidentiality clauses must be present
30. Warranty and support terms must be defined

## Quality & Compliance

31. Quality assurance and testing approach must be outlined
32. Compliance requirements (regulatory, industry standards) must be identified
33. Documentation requirements (user manuals, technical docs) must be specified
34. Training requirements and approach must be documented
35. Project closure criteria and deliverables must be defined

## Formatting & Professional Standards

36. The document must be professionally formatted with consistent styling
37. All sections must be clearly headed and numbered
38. The document must include a table of contents
39. Version control information must be present
40. Approval signatures and dates must be included
